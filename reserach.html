This page presents selected outcomes from our research, organized into key thematic areas. Each section includes representative projects and demos in multi-agent systems, reinforcement learning, robotics, machine learning, computer vision, and extended reality—showcasing both theoretical advances and applied systems.

Agent and Multi-Agent Systems

This section focuses on Agent (Multi-Agent) Reinforcement Learning for modeling human behaviours - collaborative, competitive, and mixed-agent behaviors.

This demo shows the METIS crowd simulation engine, where custom environments can be created. The platform enables realistic simulations of human movement, interactions, and behavioral patterns through multi-agent reinfocment learninh systems.

This demo presents our MAS rating framework, which evaluates agent behaviors in games using scoring models such as Elo and Glicko. It provides a fast, quantitative way to compare and rank multi-agent strategies.

This project demonstrates how collaborative behaviors are learned among warrior agents in multi-agent battle scenarios, using MARL to coordinate tactics and joint actions.

Robotics

Our research covers collaborative UAV–UGV systems with dynamic aerial-to-ground SLAM mapping and real-time data exchange; vineyard robotics using depth cameras and robotic arms for precise sample collection, dynamic leaf picking, and digital twin simulations with Gazebo; low-cost autonomous Periplus boat solutions with onboard computing and tourist storytelling; ML/CV-powered robotic arm simulations for vine leaf detection and garbage sorting with YOLO; and personalized interactive robotic fitness instructors on NAO robots leveraging pose estimation for continuous, customized guidance.

Collaborative heterogeneous robotics: dynamic mapping from aerial drones transmitted to the UGV for SLAM-based navigation, with real-time data and knowledge exchange.

UGV-mounted robotic arm and gripper for vineyard sample collection, using a depth camera for precise distance estimation to enhance manipulation and grip control.

Dynamic leaf picking with a UGV-mounted robotic arm and gripper, leveraging depth camera feedback to adapt to moving foliage in real time.

Digital twin robotics: real-time (custom) Gazebo simulation of a UGV (equipped arm), with predictive commands streamed to the physical robot for vineyard operations.

Periplus: autonomous boat navigation with integrated storytelling. A low-cost solution adaptable for every boat powered by a single-board computer, interconnected for remote monitoring and equipped with onboard tablets delivering interactive storytelling to tourists.

Simulated robotic arm for vineyard leaf detection and collection, utilizing YOL (ML/CV) for object detection and localization to guide the manipulator.

Simulated robotic arm for automated waste sorting and recycling, employing YOLO-based vision to detect and localize different types of garbage.

Personalized interactive robotic fitness instructor (NAO) integrating ML/CV pose estimation for position tracking and continuous autonomous guidance tailored to individual needs.

Machine Learning and Computer Vision

Machine Learning and Computer Vision form a core part of our research, enabling automated perception, pattern recognition, and intelligent decision-making across diverse domains. This section presents selected demonstrations showcasing applied ML systems for medical image analysis, precision agriculture, and real-world sensing tasks.

This demo applies YOLO-based object localization to identify pulmonary embolisms in CT-Scan slices, processing multiple images simultaneously. The system highlights suspicious regions with high accuracy, supporting faster and more reliable medical imaging analysis.

This demo presents our integrated ML platform for precision agriculture, combining computer vision, time-series analysis, and environmental sensing. It enables crop monitoring, disease detection, yield estimation, and automated decision support across agricultural applications.

This demo showcases a collection of earlier machine learning projects, highlighting foundational work in classification and computer vision. It reflects the evolution of our ML research through multiple experimental systems and prototypes developed over the years.
