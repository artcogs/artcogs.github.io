IBRICS

Imitating Human Behaviors by Robotic Systems through Computer Vision and Machine Learning

Abstract

IBRICS focuses on developing robotic systems that can observe human motion, understand it using computer vision and machine learning, and then reproduce similar behaviours on real robots. The project combines pose estimation, 3D motion analysis, trajectory tracking and advanced control to enable robots to perform dynamic, human-like actions in realistic environments.

By integrating deep learning, reinforcement learning and optimization-based control, IBRICS aims to build intelligent robotic agents capable of robust interaction with humans and other robots, with applications ranging from industrial settings to collaborative and assistive tasks.

Research Directions

Human Pose Extraction

Detecting and tracking human body pose from images and video as a basis for motion understanding and imitation.

3D Motion Analysis

Representing and analysing 3D human motion to capture complex, dynamic behaviours in a structured way.

Trajectory Tracking

Designing methods for accurate tracking of dynamic trajectories in 3D space for legged and humanoid robots.

Robot Control

Developing controllers that map learned motion patterns onto physical robotic platforms for agile whole-body behaviour.
