Unified No-Code Platform for Interactive Cultural Heritage Experiences

Overview

UNICHE aspires to co-design and deliver an intuitive, technology-agnostic “no-code” platform that enables cultural heritage stakeholders to effortlessly create and share high-quality interactive experiences. The platform acts as a single-entry, collaborative tool that lowers technical barriers and supports multimodal content production.

With its user-friendly environment, the platform empowers cultural heritage institutions and professionals, researchers, creative-industry freelancers, and resource-limited organisations to collaboratively generate rich interactive content in multiple formats. UNICHE prioritises ease of use and accessibility, minimising the need for specialised expertise while maximising the impact and reach of cultural heritage experiences for diverse audiences.

By leveraging advanced technologies already developed by the consortium, UNICHE provides an integrated platform comprising a Repository, Authoring Environment, Experience Applications, and an Evaluation Tool. Together, these components holistically address the “four E’s” of cultural heritage: Exhibits, Experience design, Exhibitions, and Evaluation.

Platform Components

UNICHE connects existing data spaces and advanced authoring tools into a coherent, no-code ecosystem for cultural heritage experience creation.

Repository & Data Spaces Integration

Direct access to exhibits and cultural content through the European Collaborative Cloud for Cultural Heritage (ECCCH) and related EU and national data spaces. Exhibits can be discovered, reused, and semantically enriched within UNICHE’s workflows, addressing key interoperability and accessibility challenges for cultural heritage institutions.

Authoring Environment

A no-code, collaborative authoring environment that supports AI-assisted experience design. Users can assemble narratives, multimodal content, and interaction logic without programming, focusing instead on curatorial objectives, educational goals, and storytelling quality.

Experience Apps

A suite of applications delivering onsite, hybrid, and remote experiences across devices (web, mobile, XR). Experiences can range from guided tours and interactive stories to immersive XR installations, all generated from the same underlying authoring workflow.

Evaluation & Analytics Tool

Integrated tools for collecting feedback, measuring engagement, and analysing visitor behaviour. KPIs and dashboards support continuous improvement of exhibits and experiences, closing the loop between design, deployment, and evaluation.

Pilot Sites & Open Call

UNICHE will be validated through pilot scenarios that cover diverse cultural heritage settings:

  • Open-air archaeological sites
  • Museum permanent exhibitions
  • Portable / travelling exhibitions

In a second phase, the platform will be further tested through an open call addressed to small cultural heritage institutions and creative-industry professionals. This process will foster a broader community of practice around UNICHE, support co-creation, and ensure that the platform responds to real-world needs across different cultural contexts.

ArtCogS Contributions

ArtCogS group leads several key tasks in visitor modelling, XR-based exhibition design, AI-enabled conversational agents, and analytics for personalised experiences.

T4.5 — Visitor Data Collection & Modelling

We collect, clean, and analyse visitor behaviour data from surveys, interactions, and open datasets. The resulting models reveal user preferences and feed the analytics used in later work packages.

T5.4 — XR-Based Collaborative Exhibition Design

We develop one XR tool for collaborative XR-based exhibition design. This tool let CH professionals build interactive XR exhibitions, place content in 3D space, and work together in real time.

T5.6 — AI-Based Conversational Agent

This task develops an AI-powered service that unifies visitor behaviour analytics with an LLM- and RAG-based conversational agent. The chatbot supports all UNICHE applications by offering personalised guidance and adapting to user profiles, while behavioural insights and interaction patterns feed into profiling models, real-time recommendations, dashboards, and KPIs.

T6.5 — Visitor Behaviour Analytics

In this task we develop algorithms that collect and analyse visitor-related data to identify user needs during their interactions with the project’s applications. The insights generated will support personalised experiences, adaptive system behaviour, and continuous improvement across all UNICHE tools.
